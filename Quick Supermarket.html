Quick Supermarket

Blending Offline & Online Shopping Through UX

Quick Supermarket is a mobile e-commerce application developed for a real supermarket chain aiming to enhance the in-store shopping experience with a digital touch. The key feature of the app, called Live Shopping, allows users to scan products while physically shopping in the supermarket and add them to a virtual cart for later purchase.

Project Overview

A modern mobile e-commerce solution designed for Quick Supermarket, integrating a unique Live Shopping feature to blend the in-store and digital shopping experience. This case study covers the entire UX process from research to final UI and testing.

My Role

I served as the sole UI/UX Designer for this project, responsible for the entire design lifecycle from discovery to delivery.

User Research:

Conducted interviews and surveys to understand customer behaviors and frustrations while shopping.

Wireframing

Created low-fidelity and mid-fidelity wireframes to explore app flows and feature priorities.

UI Design:

Designed a clean, intuitive mobile interface with a focus on usability and speed, especially for the Live Shopping feature.

Prototyping & Testing:

Built interactive prototypes and conducted usability testing in real world supermarket environments to ensure the app’s flows worked seamlessly under actual conditions.

L E A N UX METHEDOLOGY

For this project, I followed the Lean UX methodology to ensure fast, collaborative, and user-centered design decisions. Rather than waiting to deliver a fully polished product, Lean UX allowed me to work in rapid, iterative cycles — starting with assumptions, forming hypotheses, and testing with real users early and often. This approach was especially effective for validating the Live Shopping feature, as it involved real-world interactions in a supermarket environment. Through continuous collaboration with the client and development team, and by focusing on fast feedback loops, I was able to adapt designs quickly and deliver a solution that genuinely addressed both user needs and business objectives.
